## Privacy defaults

| Data type | Default | User can change to |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| Payment amounts | Private | Public |
| Counterparty identities | Private | Public |
| Invoice details | Private to parties | Shareable with specific parties |
| Monaris Score | Private | Shareable (Score card or API) |
| Cashflow summary | Private | Selective disclosure |
| Transaction history | Private | Verifiable proof without raw data |

---

## What is never exposed

- **Raw transaction data** to third parties without user consent.
- **Private payment details** on the public blockchain.
- **Off-chain data** to anyone other than the user and authorized connections.
- **Score factors** at individual transaction level — only aggregate signals are reflected in the Score and in what you choose to share.

Principles for documentation:

- Proofs **verify claims** without revealing underlying data.
- Proofs are generated on the user’s behalf and stored in their Monaris account.
- **Users control** which proofs are shared with which parties.
- Proofs can have **expiry dates** and can be **revoked**.

## Compliance mode

For regulated entities (e.g. banks, institutional users) that must disclose on regulatory request:

- **Compliance disclosure** does not make data public.
- **Targeted disclosure** — share only with the named regulatory authority.
- **Audit log** — full record of what was disclosed, to whom, and when.
- **User is notified** of any compliance disclosure.
