## What the cashflow picture is

The **cashflow timeline** is the Monaris Pay view of your last **90+ days** of stablecoin inflows and outflows. It is built from your connected wallet (and in V2, from off-chain sources you connect). It is categorized so you can see income, expenses, transfers, and unknowns.

---

## What you see

- **Timeline** — chronological list or chart of transactions.
- **Categories** — e.g. income, expenses, transfers, unknown (you can refine over time).
- **Counterparties** — who sent or received funds (addresses or labels where we have them).
- **Amounts** — in your chosen stablecoin (USDC, USDT, etc.).

---

## Client concentration

Monaris **detects concentration risk**: when a high share of your income comes from one source.

- We flag it when **>50% of income** in a period comes from one counterparty.
- **Mona** can comment on it (e.g. “Client A makes up X% of your income this month. That’s a concentration risk.”).
- Concentration is one of the signals that can affect your **Score** and credit readiness (diversification is better for stability).

Use this to decide whether to diversify clients or set aside reserves for delayed payments from that client.

---

## V1 vs V2

**V1 (current / in development):**

- Wallet connect + timeline
- Auto-categorization (income, expenses, transfers, unknown)
- Client concentration detection
- Mona commentary on wallet history

**V2 (planned):**

- Recurring income detection and labeling
- **Cashflow forecast** — e.g. “Based on your history, expect $X in next 30 days”
- Savings sweep rule — auto-move X% of any inflow to a savings address
- Spending category rules — limits per category
- Multi-chain support (Ethereum, Polygon, Base, Mantle — phased)

---

## How it feeds your Score

- Income consistency, volume history, and client diversity are **Score signals**.
- A clearer, more consistent cashflow picture supports a stronger Score over time.
- Mona uses the same data to give you specific recommendations (e.g. “Clear 3 more invoices to reach Tier B”).
