## Why share your Score

You can share your Monaris Score to prove cashflow history and reputation without handing over raw transaction data. Use cases: freelancer proposals, rental applications, contractor vetting, loan applications.

## V2 — external Score sharing

Planned features:

### Shareable Score card

- Generates a **verified credential** (image or link).
- Shows score, tier, and optionally a short summary (e.g. “X months of verified cashflow”).
- You choose when to generate and with whom to share.

### Public Score profile

- **monaris.co/score/[user-handle]** (or equivalent).
- Optional public page that shows a sanitized Score view (e.g. score band, tier, “verified payer” badge).
- You control whether the profile is public or unlisted.

### Selective disclosure proof

- Share only what is needed (e.g. “Score ≥ 700” or “Tier B or above”) without revealing the exact score or full history.
- Third parties can verify the claim cryptographically.

### zkTLS attestation

- Privacy-preserving proof that a third party can verify without seeing raw data.
- Useful for lenders or platforms that need to check “does this user meet our Score threshold?” without getting full Monaris data.

## Score API (B2B)

Third-party businesses can query Monaris Scores for their counterparties via API, **only if the user has granted permission**. Documentation for the Score API is in the [API section](/api/score-api). It covers:

- Authentication and API key management
- Query endpoint: `GET /score/{wallet_address}` (or equivalent)
- Response schema: score, tier, confidence level, last updated
- Consent requirement: user must have allowed their Score to be queried
- Pricing: per-query or monthly subscription (see [Revenue model](/reference/roadmap#revenue) or product pricing)

---

## What is never shared without consent

- Raw transaction data
- Individual invoice or payment details (unless you explicitly share them)
- Off-chain data (bank, accounting) used in Score calculation
- Score factors at single-transaction level — only aggregate signals are reflected in the Score and in what you choose to share
