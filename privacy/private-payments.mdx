## Overview

All payments processed through the **Monaris Settlement Router** are **private by default**. Counterparties, amounts, and payment patterns are shielded from public blockchain visibility unless you explicitly choose to make them transparent.

This is part of **Secrets as a Service** — programmable privacy controls for cashflows.

---

## The problem this solves

Public blockchain transactions expose:

- **Who is paying whom** — counterparty visibility
- **Exact amounts** — financial pattern visibility
- **Payment timing and frequency** — behavioral visibility
- **Business relationships** — competitive intelligence

A freelancer receiving $3,000/month from a US company may not want that relationship, amount, and timing visible to the whole internet. A business paying vendors may not want competitors to see supplier relationships.

---

## The Monaris Privacy Router

All payments sent **through Monaris Pay** go through the **Privacy Router** by default.

```
USER INITIATES PAYMENT
        │
        ▼
PRIVACY ROUTER (Monaris Settlement Layer)
        │
        ├─ Strips counterparty metadata from public chain visibility
        ├─ Routes through privacy-preserving settlement path
        ├─ Generates a private receipt (visible only to sender + recipient)
        ├─ Anchors a proof-of-payment on-chain (verifiable but not readable)
        └─ Updates Monaris Score with verified payment (private signal)
        │
        ▼
RECIPIENT RECEIVES PAYMENT
(amount visible only to recipient, not public)
```

---

## What is private by default

- **Counterparty** — who you paid / who paid you (not visible on public chain).
- **Amount** — not visible on public chain in readable form.
- **Timing and frequency** — payment pattern is not exposed publicly.

---

## What still happens

- **Recipient** receives the full amount and can see the payment in their Monaris account (or via their wallet, depending on implementation).
- **Private receipt** — stored for you and the recipient; not public.
- **Proof-of-payment** — anchored on-chain so the payment is **verifiable** (e.g. by you, recipient, or with selective disclosure) without being **readable** by the public.
- **Monaris Score** — updated internally with the verified payment. Score data is internal; we do not publish it.

---

## Opt-in transparency

You can choose to make a specific payment **public** (transparent on-chain) if you want. Transparency is **opt-in**, not opt-out.
