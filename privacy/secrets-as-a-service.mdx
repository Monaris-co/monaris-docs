## What Secrets as a Service is

**Secrets as a Service** is Monaris’s programmable privacy feature set. You define rules for what stays private, what can be disclosed, and to whom — at the transaction or account level. Privacy and verifiability are not opposites: you can keep a payment private while still proving that it happened, or that an amount was within a range, for a lender or tax authority.

## Programmable privacy rules — examples

You can define rules that run automatically:

- **"All payments to Vendor A are private"**
- **"Amounts above $5,000 are always private"**
- **"Payments between 9pm–6am are private"**
- **"All payroll payments are private — but generate a verifiable receipt"**
- **"Public by default, except when paying category = personal"**
- **"All payments private — but Score data is shareable"**

---

## Selective disclosure

**Privacy and verifiability are not opposites.**

You can keep a payment fully private on-chain and still:

- Generate a **cryptographic proof** that the payment happened (for the recipient).
- Prove the amount was **within a range** (for a lender checking income).
- Prove **on-time payment** (for Score calculation).
- Generate a **verifiable receipt** (for tax purposes).

You control what is **secret** and what is **provable**, separately.

---

## Compliance mode

For regulated entities (e.g. banks, institutional users) that need to disclose on regulatory request:

- **Compliance disclosure** does not make data public.
- **Targeted disclosure** — share only with the named regulatory authority.
- **Audit log** — full record of what was disclosed, to whom, and when.
- You are **notified** of any compliance disclosure.
