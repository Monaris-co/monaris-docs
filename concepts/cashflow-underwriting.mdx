## Definition

**Cashflow underwriting** is the practice of assessing credit risk based on **income reliability**, **payment patterns**, and **cashflow history** — rather than collateral or traditional credit scores (e.g. FICO).

---

## Why it matters

In the stablecoin economy, many users and businesses have **no traditional credit file**. Banks and legacy lenders rely on FICO, collateral, or manual underwriting. Monaris uses **on-chain and (in V2) off-chain cashflow** to build a verifiable picture: how much you earn, how often, from whom, and whether you pay on time. That picture becomes a **credit signal** — the Monaris Score — which can unlock credit without requiring crypto collateral.

---

## How it differs from traditional credit

| Traditional | Cashflow underwriting (Monaris) |
|-------------|----------------------------------|
| FICO, bureau data | Score from verified inflows/outflows, invoice behavior, repayment history |
| Collateral (house, car, crypto) | Income and payment behavior; repayment from future inflows |
| Static snapshot | Dynamic: Score updates as behavior and history change |
| Opaque factors | Explainable: which signals help or hurt your Score |

---

## What we use (signals)

- **Invoice completion rate** — % of issued invoices that get paid
- **Payment timeliness** — % paid on or before due date
- **Income consistency** — regularity and predictability of inflows
- **Client diversity** — number of distinct paying counterparties
- **Volume history** — total verified volume over time
- **Repayment behavior (V2)** — on-time repayment of Monaris Credit
- **Off-chain (V2)** — bank, accounting, payroll (with consent)

---

## The sequencing rule

Monaris never offers credit until **verifiable history** exists: minimum 90 days of cashflow history, minimum Score threshold (e.g. 700 for Credit), minimum volume. **Score before credit. Always.**
