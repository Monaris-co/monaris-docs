## The problem: public by default

On most blockchains, **every transaction is public**. Anyone can see:

- **Who sent** and **who received** (addresses)
- **Amount** and **asset type**
- **When** and **how often**

For individuals and businesses, that means:

- Income and spending patterns are visible to competitors, scrapers, and strangers.
- Business relationships (who pays whom) are exposed.
- Financial behavior can be inferred and used without your consent.

---

## Why that matters for stablecoins

Stablecoins are used for **payroll**, **invoicing**, **B2B payments**, and **remittances**. Real-world identities can be linked to addresses (e.g. via KYC, exchanges, or patterns). So "public money" can mean **public financial life** — which many users do not want.

---

## How Monaris addresses it

### Privacy Router

All payments sent **through Monaris Pay** go through the **Privacy Router** by default. Counterparties and amounts are **not** visible on the public chain in a readable form. A **proof-of-payment** is anchored so the payment is verifiable without exposing details to the world.

### Secrets as a Service

You set **programmable privacy rules**: which payments are private, which can be disclosed, and to whom. You can keep a payment private and still generate **selective disclosure proofs** (e.g. "I paid at least $X" or "I have on-time payment history") for a lender or auditor.

### Principle

**Privacy and verifiability are not opposites.** Monaris gives you both: control over what is secret and the ability to prove what is needed.

---

## What stays private (by default)

- Payment amounts (on public chain)
- Counterparty identities (on public chain)
- Payment timing and frequency (as readable data)

What you **can** do: share a proof with a specific party, use compliance mode for regulators, or opt in to transparency for specific transactions.
